IT Tech: Cloud, Services and Servers

 

What does it do?

 

Popularized by Amazon in the early 2000’s, cloud computing has enabled countless large and small scale organisations to deliver resources to it’s employees and/or customers in a scalable solution that will ultimately save companies valuable income in the long run. By establishing a system developed with cloud computing in mind, these organisations will no longer need to inaugurate schemes of hardware to which need to be physically wired and lifted to areas that require a large space to operate fundamentally increasing costs on space, runtime and more physical labour.

 

Forming a cloud-based solution removes various issues that many organisations faced in the nineties and the early 2000’s. However, how does this system actually function? The resources owned by a specific company are hosted on a physical server typically on the premises of the organisation’s branch or headquarters and in order for an individual to access the information being hosted, they utilise the internet.

 

This is a beneficial relationship between both customer and company as the customer is not required to download or install any pieces of software (typically) in order to gain access to the cloud solution and as already discussed, companies are able to save funds which can be invested in other factions of their organisation.

 

There are variations of this cloud-based system that are suitable to differing situations. For example, the most predominantly utilised cloud service is found within Google that includes applications such as emails, word documents, drive (store any type of data) and various other useful apps. However for more professional usage, there are other distinct networks that enable platforms for users to develop information technology software that you would commonly see in a business sense.

 

According to Amazon Web Services, there are three model types of cloud computing solutions and these are; Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Software as a Service (SaaS). Discussing IaaS, it provides the largest amount of control over resources within a cloud system and allows users to gain access to virtual or dedicated hardware within the host. With PaaS, it is less complex and allows individuals to focus on application deployment and finally with SaaS, it provides only the application-side of the system to the individual such as Google’s web-based email service (Gmail).

 

A minor inconvenience that some users may find with typical cloud systems would be the little amount of data that is able to be stored in these systems. As companies are required to serve countless customers they need to segment data appropriately for each user and if an individual requires a larger portion of storage capacity they would be obligated to purchase the space.

 

With cloud computing dominating the information technology social market as of late it would not be a far stretch to say that it will continue to grow and evolve. Currently physical servers are still used to store sensitive information within large and small scale organisations so an obvious goal would be to slowly phase out physical servers and to become completely dependant on the cloud. However one significant risk to this would be the fact that cloud services are extensively difficult to maintain high levels of security and many organisations would currently opt to keep their data safe and secure physically than risk having it on the cloud.

 

 

 

 

 

 

What is the likely impact?

 

If in a perfect world, companies and other organisations were able to develop the cloud computing and cloud systems further to a point where physical servers that store sensitive data were no longer required it would ultimately allow the corporate side of the relationship to once again save further revenue as server space is no longer necessary and run time costs would also decline further saving the company revenue.

 

However these costs that were once used for physical server space and runtime would be re-invested into the development of the cloud system and also investing in high levels of security to encrypt and storage of the sensitive data.

 

With the reduction of physical servers it would vastly decrease the requirement for individuals who have occupations that fall in line with this work and ultimately making them redundant such as networking technicians. However networking engineers and perhaps some advanced technicians would be able to develop skills in the field of cloud security to maintain job security which alters the scope of the information technology workforce.

 

How will this affect you?

 

For something as significant as moving physical servers onto the cloud, this hardly affects the regular everyday user. This vastly affects the stakeholders of the corporate companies that opt for full-cloud integration systems as it is up to these individuals to maintain and monitor this system. As for the end users, they should not see any differences with this distinct integration, cloud services are to perform as per usual which places the pressure and burden on the cloud service providers to be able to deliver high levels of intuitiveness and efficiency.

 

If anything, the fact that servers may also be placed onto the cloud themselves may also vastly increase overall speed and competence of the system as a whole. Individuals gaining access to the cloud network would no longer have to be directed to a physical server at this point making the path that information travels to the end user stream-lined which not only can be seen as a positive but also gives malicious users a direct line to intercept said data.

 

 



 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

IT Tech: Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies

What Does It Do?

The blockchain is the latest frontier in the world of currency transfer. It takes the concept of regulated banks, cuts out the middleman, and acts as its own decentralised system which is owned by not one authority, but controlled and regulated by millions of independent users across the globe. Blockchain is the backbone of cryptocurrency transfer, as it is essentially a public online ledger for transactions.

 

The formats of currency distributed via the blockchain are known as cryptocurrencies. Cryptocurrencies use cryptographic protocols in order to encrypt sensitive data transfers, to secure their units of exchange. This enables the data regarding transfer of cryptocurrencies to be practically impossible to extract, or for people to illegally duplicate the currency, and also protects the personal data of the users involved in the transaction.

 

The first cryptocurrency was created in late 2008, by Satoshi Nakamoto, the unknown creator of Bitcoin, the original, the most popular and most valuable cryptocurrency. Satoshi intended to create merely a ‘Peer-to-peer electronic cash system’ with the creation of Bitcoin. Although in the past there had been countless attempts to create a purely electronic cash system, those attempts had failed. What made Satoshi’s invention stand out from the crowd was what would become the blockchain - a decentralised system which allow users to be in full control of their money. Currently, as of September 2018, there are roughly 2000 reported cryptocurrencies in public circulation.

 

But how does one procure cryptocurrencies? Within this world of highly-encrypted digital transactions, regular currencies are referred to as ‘Fiat’ currency. Dedicated organisations provide services to transfer regular currencies into a range of cryptos, which can then be entered into online exchanges which are a home to thousands of other cryptos to be bought, sold, and traded via the blockchain.

 

In theory, this all sounds revolutionary. However, as expected, due to the crypto market being incredibly new, it is not yet a foolproof system. The inherent digital aspect of the format, as well as its current infancy, leaves it vulnerable to attacks, such as phishing. Phishing is a very common scam which can occur with cryptocurrency & blockchain related transactions, as hackers can replicate websites used to mediate these transactions (such as BTC Markets for Australia), put this copied version under a very similar appearing URL, and trick users into entering their highly sensitive financial details directly to a scammer’s bank account. Negative experiences such as these during such a pivotal time period in cryptocurrencies/the blockchain’s period of establishment can create a lot of negative press, and prevent many users from adopting its technologies. This situation can be likened to the current state of the self driving car industry, as death caused by these cars are far less common than that of human-caused accidents, however, the press created from a self-driving related death is far more dramatic and widespread than that of the latter.

 

 

 

 

 

 

What Is The Likely Impact?

The blockchain and cryptocurrencies have the capability to turn the entire financial industry on its head, revolutionise the way consumers think about money, and turn the entire banking industry to irrelevancy. However, the questionable and intimidating reputation of these components lead a lot of people to be reluctant to convert their life savings into cryptocurrencies.

 

One very appealing fact of this volatility of the cryptocurrency market is its ability to enable investors to make (or lose) colossal gains in wealth. For example, in December 2017, Bitcoin reached a peak of ~$26,000 (per bitcoin). As of now (September 2018), Bitcoin is valued at ~$8000 - yet, this doesn’t negate the very real possibility that Bitcoin (as well as the countless other cryptocurrencies) could shoot up to astronomical heights of value again.  

 

Correspondingly, the blockchain’s inherent concept of decentralised currency transfer will have an undeniable impact on almost all financial operations throughout the world, if it comes into greater relevance. “A purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash would allow online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without going through a financial institution.” These are the words of Satoshi Nakamoto, Bitcoin’s pseudonymous creator. IT Tech: Autonomous Vehicles

What Does It Do?

As the name suggests, an autonomous vehicle is a car or other form of transport that will perform the task of transporting an individual from ‘Point: A’ to ‘Point: B’ without the need of a driver for these said vehicles. This type of project is something that has seen recent developments within the last few years with many large scale organisations building their own solution of an autonomous vehicle, otherwise known as ‘driverless cars’, ‘robot cars’ or ‘self-driving cars’.

 

Many large scale organisations such as General Motors, Ford, BMW, Google and many more have opted in autonomous vehicle research and development. Some may ask, why would you want a driverless car? This can be answered a few ways; first of all it is insanely awesome that technology has come this far to where you do not need to drive or require a driver to get you between two destinations. Additionally, this allows individuals to continue other tasks such as working, studying or sleeping instead of wasting countless hours over the course of a year driving. Furthermore, this type of technology can be utilised as a service to which produces customers for your organisation. One of the largest companies in the service industry, Uber have established an autonomous vehicle that enables Uber to have one less employee that requires payment or salary.

 

The complexity of creating a solution that suffices to abide by our current road laws as well as keeping both passengers and pedestrians safe is immense. Autonomous vehicles work in largely the same way from developer-to-developer in that sensors are utilised around the vehicle to construct an internal map of the current surroundings via lasers. In addition to these sensors, some developers have opted to include radars and high powered cameras within their system for further security.

 

Furthermore, complicated software has to be refined to process the mapping of the surroundings and to deliver signals to the so called ’actuators’ of the vehicle to gain control of steering, braking and accelerating along the planned direction of the carrier. However when the software of the system malfunctions it would need human interaction to enable the vehicle to proceed safely. According to Google’s current statistics on their solution, since their product has been on the roads providing imagery for Google Earth that up until 2016 they have only had 14 collisions of which 13 of those incidents were to the fault of other drivers on the road (there was unfortunately a software malfunction that caused a crash in 2016).

 

In the current climate of driverless vehicles there has always been an element of mystery in the fact that if there was ever a defect in the system’s software then human intervening would be necessary (aside from Google’s current in-house product). The next steps forward to develop this solution further is to eliminate the requirement of a driver being present within the vehicle. If by chance the software and sensors are to a grade of quality that exceeds human skills it goes towards a more significant goal of reducing the road death toll to lower numbers which benefits society in an immensely positive way.

Cyber Security

What Does It Do?

Cyber security as the name suggests is the application of security processes onto cyber (or technological) platforms. This can branch to various natures such as network security which focuses on halting malicious users in intercepting internet or data traffic within a network system. Another faction of cyber security includes application security to protect applications from outside sources through usage of an array of software and hardware such as firewalls and IP blockers to negate entry of specified users.

 

Information security (infosec) is vital confidential data that demands high levels of security therefore a set of processes and policies allow the confidentiality, integrity and availability of the information to remain intact. The most common approach that malicious individuals attempt to abduct this data is through applying malware, ransomware or by phishing the information from the victim, either way, the goal of these users are to learn confidential intelligence about the victim. People can rely on high levels of security through the means of complex password structures, encryption key management and detection systems such as ‘host intrusion’ and ‘network intrusion’ systems enable unauthorised users to be identified and be shunned via usage of firewalls, spyware prevention and antivirus applications. Additionally, there is another form of cyber security titled operation security (opsec). Through utilising the steps of opsec information assets can be identified and protected correctly and efficiently;

 

1.     Identify critical information: Pinpoint the types of files and documents that could hinder the organisation if it were to fall into opposing hands.

 

2.     Determine threats: Discuss who or the types of individuals that pose a direct threat to the organisation and ensure they cannot gain access to the systems, such as hackers or competing organisations.

 

3.     Analyse vulnerabilities: Classify weak points within the organisation’s systems and/or network that could be prone to attacks.

 

4.     Assess risks: Following the classification of the system’s weak points individuals are required to determine threat levels and how likely an attack from that angle could occur.

 

5.     Apply appropriate countermeasures: Enact a proposed plan to patch up weaknesses within the organisation’s system with improvements to hardware or software or applying training to employees.

 

If an organisation can correctly apply the steps proposed in the operation security planning then it would vastly improve their chances of maintaining integrity of their sensitive data.

 

With the continued improvement of technology, both sides of the argument being the corporates and the malicious individuals will grow and evolve with new ways to attack and prevent organisational attacks. According to statistics from Accenture, cyber crime cost organisations a total of $11.7 million throughout the year of 2017 which is surprising considering the fact that this has grown 23% in comparison to 2016, showing that cyber crime is immensely important in preventing and why development of further systems is required.

 

One of the most difficult and frustrating components of cyber attacks is the fact that a majority of times the attacks occur overseas on external devices, according to an infographic on Varonis, in 2017, 20% of attacks were sourced from China and 11% were sourced from the United States of America. However individuals wishing to gain unauthorised access to systems are able to utilise virtual private networks (VPNs) to mask their actual location thus hindering the ability to locate these malicious people.

What Is The Likely Impact?

Continued research and development into furthering the capabilities of cyber security is essential and should be at the forefront of most large and even small scale organisations as within this technological era protecting confidential data is as important as it has ever been. Mischievous individuals targeting organisations or personal devices will continue to evolve in ways in which they are able to extract information and files and being able to keep up with their processes will be vital for future endeavours.

 

With such a critical occupation being so in demand it will enable countless jobs in the current climate and in the near future and is the field of work in which I would personally recommend many individuals to gain knowledge in and apply themselves to. Considering there are various types of cyber security, it enables students and other people aspiring an occupation in the cyber security sector to have options available to move between departments adding variety to their jobs.

 

How Will This Affect You?

 

For not only myself, but for the greater majority of society, we will all continuously be required to purchase and upgrade our antivirus software and spyware protection as the intent to steal or gain information will continue to rise. As stated previously this should be the forefront of organisation’s to-do lists as they are considered primary targets by these thieves.

 

Considering the fact that my current studies are in the field of information technology, many paths are available to access depending on what occupation I am striving for. With cyber security at the pinnacle of importance in the current climate it is a safe bet to apply myself with this industry for not only decent salary and job security, but to also experience first-hand the development of technology before our eyes.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Project Idea

Motivation

 

The world is overflowed with wastes and trash and the concept of recycling is still not widely used. However, that is about to change. The ‘Trashocated’ application allows users to find their nearest recycle bins as well as rewarding them when doing so. How this works is that first, it alerts users of their nearest recycle bins. Then, When the users correctly dispose of their trash, The Trashocated app gives them reward points. With those points they can shop from the Trashocated app store which contains products that are made only with recycled items. If the user chooses to, they can donate their points for charitable causes. In the case where the user is unable to drop off their trash at the special trash can locations, volunteers will be assigned to pick up the trash from the location that both the user and volunteer have chosen. The user can choose to become a premium member and receive a mini home assistant. The home assistant is complimentary but not compulsory for this application to work but it is highly recommended as it has many benefits.

 

Description

 

The ‘Trashocated’ application works to increase recycling amongst people by making the process much simpler. The application has two main goals. First to get users to dispose their trash correctly and secondly, reward them with points when they successfully do so. The application has a locating feature installed that will identify the person’s location and estimate the nearest recycle bin to them. With that it will estimate the distance between the two and give information about the fastest route to get to the place. The application also has a “To -do” function installed where the user enters the dates and times of when they wish to dispose of their garbage. The application will send a reminder when it is time to do that task. The application is also synced with the weather application to ensure that the weather is suitable to go out.

 

Another benefit of the installed to do list is that it is compatible with other calendar and to do list installed on the phone. The application will analyse to see when the person is free based on their schedules and it will the available dates and times to the user as a list when they open the “To- do” page. If the user changes or adds a plan to their schedule in another application but that date is the same date to which they had planned to recycle their trash, Trashocated will notify the user that there has been an overlap on that date and to make changes. It will also suggest the closest available date and time to the date and time they had previously chosen. In case the person does not have the time to go to the bins, the application will recommend some pre-chosen locations to the user. The pre-chosen locations are locations where the volunteers can access easily. If the recommendations are acceptable by the user, they will click on the “okay” button. Then the volunteer that is assigned to that area will be notified to collect and dispose of the trash. Alternatively, the user can choose their preferred location by dropping a pin on the location they wish to leave their trash and the first volunteer to accept that request will be assigned to dispose of the trash.

 

If the user had the mini home assistant installed, they can choose to allow the volunteers to either enter their home and collect their trash from inside their house or pick up the trash they have left outside the house. The mini home assistant will be very useful in this case as it will be synced with the security cameras of the user’s house.

 

The volunteers will write the exact date and time they have entered the house through the application and they will use their special code to activate the camera to take a picture of them entering and exiting the house and they will send it to the user via the Trashocated app to ensure them that the person who has entered is from the company and is not an unwanted guest. However, in any of the cases where the user uses the volunteers to dispose of their trash, the reward point will be lessened from the user and will be added to the volunteer’s account. The users are also able to choose the volunteer they are most comfortable with instead of having the application assign one to them.

Another benefit of the mini assistant is that it will be synced with the user’s smart fridge. It will identify the potential recyclable items that are in the fridge and will list it in the application. This will make the process to identify recyclable items much easier to the users and it will also decrease the chances of the user forgetting to recycle an item.

 

Moreover, the mini assistant will be a great help to the elderly as it will have speech recognition installed and the user can assign volunteers to come and collect their trash by only speaking to the home assistant. The home assistant will also notify users 10 mins before the volunteers arrive to ensure that the user is still happy to have volunteers from the company enter their homes.

 

To reach the goal and purpose of this application the users’ behaviour to dispose properly of the garbage must be continues. Hence, the reward and store section are added to this application. The application is notified vie censors when the user discards of their rubbish accordingly and will add points to their account. There will be a list of items in the store section which includes clothing, housewares and stationary items all of which are made with recycled material. This action will slowly help the users’ behaviour to change from buying substances made from raw material to buying recycled items through the Translocated company.

 

Moreover, the app is also beneficial to charities and other in need matters. There are several different charity organizations which have an agreement with the Translocated company. The agreement suggests that not only will the Translocated company financially assist to those charity organizations, but the users of the company will also be given the choice to donate as they see fit through the reward system of the application. This process will promote humanity as well motivate others to join.

 

The application also consists of minor elements. One of the less highlighted part of the application is the volunteering opportunities it will create for people. The volunteers will learn through making a difference and seeing firsthand the difference their work has made. The volunteers will be designated to pick up the trash left by users at the alternative locations and they will also be given the opportunity to visit big charitable organizations and comprehend the way those organizations work. Special perks will be given to volunteers as well as rewards, additionally they will also be given the freedom to choose when they want to work since there is no set time to when the users will drop off their garbage. Therefore, they will gain unique experience without being under the pressure to work in specific times.
